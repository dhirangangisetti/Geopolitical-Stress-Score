Geopolitical Stress Score — Calculation Pipeline

How 11 global assets become one composite stress number

Raw Daily Price Data
Historical closing prices fetched via Yahoo Finance from 1 January 2000 to present. All data is free and public. Assets with shorter histories (Bitcoin from 2014, High Yield Bonds from 2007, Emerging Markets from 2003) contribute once available.

Stage 2 — Transform
Daily Returns
Prices are converted to daily percentage returns so assets on different scales (e.g. gold at $2,000 vs VIX at 20) can be compared on equal footing. VIX is the exception — its raw level is used directly because a VIX of 40 is inherently meaningful, not just a percentage move in VIX.
returnt = (pricet − pricet-1) / pricet-1     [all assets except VIX]
VIX: raw level used as-is
Stage 3 — Normalise
Rolling 252-Day Z-Score per Asset
For each asset on each trading day, the z-score measures how unusual today's return is relative to the past 252 trading days (one full trading year). The window rolls forward daily, so "normal" always reflects recent market conditions, not a static historical average. A minimum of 252 days of prior data is required before a z-score is produced.
zt = (returnt − rolling_mean252) / rolling_std252

z ≈ 0  →  ordinary day
z > 2  →  unusual
z > 3  →  extreme
Stage 4 — Align
Direction Multiplier Applied
Each asset's z-score is multiplied by +1 or −1 so that a positive z-score always means stress, regardless of the asset. For example, a falling S&P 500 produces a negative return and a negative z-score — multiplying by −1 flips it positive. This ensures all 11 signals point in the same direction before averaging. No subjective weights are applied at this stage or any other.
adjusted_z = raw_z × direction_multiplier
  S&P 500, Bitcoin, EEM, HYG, EUR/USD, JPY: × −1
  Gold, Oil, VIX, USD Index: × +1
  Treasury Yield: × −1  (falling yield = stress)
Stage 5 — Composite
Equal-Weight Average → GSS Score
The 11 direction-adjusted z-scores are averaged with equal weight into the final Geopolitical Stress Score. No asset is treated as more important than another. A minimum of 6 out of 11 assets must have valid z-scores on a given day for the composite to be computed — this handles the Bitcoin data gap cleanly before 2014.
z₁ + z₂ + z₃ + ··· + z₁₁ ÷ n GSS Score
No weights, by design. Assigning weights (e.g. gold at 20%, VIX at 15%) would require defending arbitrary choices. Equal weighting means the score rises when multiple assets simultaneously deviate from normal — which is the definition of systemic stress. A single asset spiking in isolation barely moves the composite.
