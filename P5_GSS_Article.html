Is it really that bad right now?
25 years of market shocks, laid flat.

Trade wars. Tariff shocks. Geopolitical fractures. The news cycle treats all of it as unprecedented. But markets have a memory — and 25 years of price data tells a more complicated story.

I built this to put the current moment in context. Not to draw conclusions. Not to say things are fine. Just to see what global markets — equities, bonds, currencies, commodities, and crypto — actually did the last time the world felt like this. And the time before that.

Under the hood

Every day, for each asset, I calculate how far that day's price move sits from the average of the prior 252 trading days — expressed in standard deviations. That's the z-score. A reading near zero is a normal day. A reading above two means something genuinely unusual happened for that asset in that period.

The stress-adjusted toggle in the Events tab flips the sign on assets that fall under stress (equities, credit) so that a high reading always points in the same direction — up means abnormal, regardless of which way the price moved. This is a convention based on historical patterns. It holds most of the time. Not always.
